1. PRIMARY TRANSACTIONAL DATASET: UPSTREAM SUPPLY CHAIN & LOGISTICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Source: Kaggle Supply Chain Operations & Logistics Matrix (Anonymized Global Enterprise)
* Data Footprint: Core operational tracking log containing logistical milestones.
* Relational Role: Serves as the Master Ledger for physical inventory movement.

Key Features & Database Fields Tracked:
- Product Details: SKU IDs, core product categories (Cosmetics, Haircare, Skincare).
- Cost Structures: Baseline manufacturing costs, freight costs, and inventory values.
- Operational Nodes: Supplier IDs (Mapping Supplier 1 through Supplier 5).
- Quality Metrics: Batch inspection pass/fail records, customer defect percentages.
- Logistics Variables: Order quantities, historical stock levels, shipping modes (Air, Road, Rail, Sea).
- Lead Time Metrics: Scheduled supplier lead times vs. actual carrier delivery lead times.

Strategic Utility:
Used to isolate critical bottleneck variables, compute lead-time planning variances, 
and flag severe supplier quality failures (such as the 66.7% inspection failure 
rate isolated on Supplier 4).


2. CONSUMER SENTIMENT DATASET: DOWNSTREAM DEMAND-SENSING REGISTRY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Source: Kaggle Sephora Products and Consumer Reviews Database
* Data Footprint: 1.09 Million rows of historical customer transaction & review logs.
* Relational Role: Serves as the real-time marketplace demand proxy.

Key Features & Database Fields Tracked:
- Product Registry: 12,000+ distinct Sephora SKUs, brand mappings, and ingredients.
- Market Metrics: Retail prices, primary categories, popularity scores, and online love counts.
- Consumer Sentiment: 1.09M rows of text reviews, star ratings, and transactional timestamps.
- Temporal Anchors: Review submission years and months (used to model velocity trends).

Strategic Utility:
By processing 1.09 million consumer interaction timestamps, this dataset is 
transformed into an active "demand-sensing" signal. It allows an S&OP planning 
team to predict inventory depletion on store shelves up to 30 days before a 
traditional warehouse stockout appears on the ledger.


3. ENGINEERED CROSS-DATASET INTEGRATION (DATA BRIDGE LOGIC)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Because raw public datasets lack native corporate integration keys, the following 
enterprise data architectural strategies were deployed to bridge the data:

A. The Category Bridge Key:
   An explicit relational bridge was built using SQL to join the operational 
   Supply Chain table with the 1.09M-row Sephora table at the product category 
   level (Cosmetics, Haircare, Skincare). This allows upstream logistics parameters 
   to dynamically adjust based on downstream consumer review velocity.

B. Structural Attribute Proxy Strategy (Geopolitical Risk Mitigation):
   To model global macro trade risk without explicit origin tracking codes, 
   a dynamic "Tariff Impact Coefficient" was engineered directly into the data 
   schema based on product types. This provides the C-suite with a functional 
   CFO simulation tool to model a 25% margin compression penalty across high-risk 
   imported cosmetic SKUs.

C. Null Value Remediation & Data Governance:
   Heavy database cleaning routines dropped corrupt, non-reporting fields 
   (such as 'value_price_usd' which carried a >94% null rate) to ensure absolute 
   data integrity before pushing the final data payload into Tableau executive 
   visualization environments.
